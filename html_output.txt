          <p class="break_word" itemprop="reviewBody">
           Catching up with close friends always makes a great excuse to dive deep into the cellar.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Menu: Roasted beets, spring mix, feta, pistachios &amp; balsamic vinaigrette, select cuts of prime filet, tomahawk &amp; wagyu ribeye with a side of roasted mashed sweet potatoes.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Mousse Fils Perpetuelle BdN L'OR D'Eugene &amp; 2021 Cornell Chardonnay.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Side by side 2005 Domaine Jean Louis Chave Hermitage &amp; 1995 Abreu Madrona Ranch.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           2010 Vieux Chateau Certan
           <br/>
           <br/>
           2009 Dolce Winery Late Harvest
           <br/>
           <br/>
           1995 Madrona- this wine is in a beautiful spot. One of the prettiest pale ruby reds with no edges. Just stunning to look. There is still a touch of sweet red currant fruits on the mature palate. Almost no sediment. Potpourri. I believe this wine would continue to age gracefully for another 5 years before starting a new chapter. Having the JLC side by side was the perfect compliment of finesse &amp; power.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           13.4 ABV
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Easily a 95 in my book.
